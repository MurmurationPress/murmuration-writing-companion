# Writing Companion visual smoke test

Use a disposable vault with representative manuscript and Story World content. Before starting, record the vault diff and the state of editorial storage.

## Themes and three-pane composition

1. Open the Manuscript or Story World navigator on the left, a Markdown note or timeline in the centre, and Writing Companion on the right.
2. Repeat in Obsidian's default light and dark themes. Confirm text, links, muted metadata, focus rings, warnings and selected rows remain readable without a custom theme.
3. Confirm both sidebars feel lighter than the centre workspace and that hiding either sidebar does not change the remaining pane's layout authority.

## Navigators and inspector

4. In Manuscript, inspect several books, nested parts and long scene names. Confirm rows remain compact, hierarchy is clear, the active scene is restrained, and move controls appear on hover or keyboard focus.
5. Narrow the Manuscript navigator. Confirm titles truncate deliberately, controls remain usable, and keyboard focus is visible.
6. In Story World, inspect populated and empty searches, long entity names, dates, statuses, group counts, create controls and the timeline entry point. Confirm the active row is clear without looking like an accent-filled browser button.
7. Select entities with long summaries, several scopes and sources. Confirm identity, kind and status lead the right inspector while metadata wraps cleanly in a narrow sidebar.

## Relationships and event time

8. Inspect valid, incomplete and unresolved relationships. Confirm readable sentences remain primary, canon status is quiet, and unresolved assertions are distinguishable without fatal-error styling.
9. Exercise Add, Edit, Mark superseded and Remove through their existing confirmation flows. Confirm actions become clearer on hover/focus and advanced qualifiers remain progressively disclosed.
10. Open the event-time editor for point, range and preserved unsupported values. Confirm labels, date/time controls, preview, primary confirmation and Cancel align with the relationship form at wide and narrow inspector widths.

## Timeline workspace

11. Open Chronology with exact points, ranges, unsupported values and undated events. Confirm the temporal spine is clear; titles, dates, metadata, relative intervals and sources have distinct hierarchy; and section differences do not rely on colour alone.
12. Open Event–scene map with long event/scene names, shared scenes, several sources and unresolved sources. Confirm connections and selected nodes remain legible and keyboard focus visibly follows interactive nodes.
13. Test a wide centre pane with both sidebars open, then a narrow split pane. Confirm cards and nodes use available width, labels do not collapse into one-word columns, and no right-inspector width constraint leaks into the timeline.

## Accessibility and authority

14. Navigate all controls using Tab and Shift-Tab at normal and increased zoom. Confirm focus is always visible and targets remain practical on a high-density display.
15. Enable reduced motion and confirm no essential state depends on transition or animation.
16. Resize panes, change filters, switch timeline presentation, expand details and inspect records without confirming an edit. Compare against the initial record and confirm no Markdown, manuscript prose or editorial-store data changed.
